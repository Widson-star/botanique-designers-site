## 1. Product identity and positioning

- **GardenCare is a maintenance programme offered by Botanique Designers.** It is
  **not** a separate company, legal entity, subsidiary or unrelated brand. It has
  no independent ownership, banking, branding or contact identity of its own.
- All GardenCare work is performed and invoiced under Botanique Designers.
- Positioning: a structured, agreement-based ongoing garden-care programme for
  properties within the launch coverage area, built on the practice's existing
  horticultural and landscape expertise.
- GardenCare is an **ongoing-care** offering. It is distinct from — and does not
  replace or absorb — design, landscape architecture, implementation/construction,
  or specialist horticultural advisory work.

## 2. Intended customers

- Residential homeowners with lawns and gardens who want scheduled, reliable
  upkeep under a written agreement.
- Estate and gated-community homes, and estate/common-area managers, within the
  coverage area.
- Small commercial, institutional and hospitality grounds where the scope suits a
  recurring maintenance agreement (larger or complex commercial grounds may be
  handled under a bespoke commercial arrangement rather than a standard GardenCare
  structure — to be confirmed in Phase B).
- Customers who have had a garden designed/installed (by Botanique or others) and
  want it maintained to a defined standard.

Customers outside the launch coverage area, or whose needs are primarily design,
installation, rehabilitation or specialist work, are **not** GardenCare customers
and should be routed to the relevant service enquiry instead.

## 3. Launch coverage (precise, approved wording)

> **Nairobi Metropolitan Area — Nairobi City and selected accessible locations in
> Kiambu, Kajiado and Machakos counties, subject to site assessment and route
> availability.**

Coverage rules:

- Do **not** imply that every location throughout Kiambu, Kajiado or Machakos is
  automatically covered. Coverage in the neighbouring counties is limited to
  **selected accessible locations**, and is always **subject to site assessment
  and route availability**.
- Coverage is **not** nationwide and is **not** regional. GardenCare launch
  coverage is the Nairobi Metropolitan Area only, as worded above.
- This coverage statement applies to the **GardenCare programme**. It does not
  restrict or alter the practice's separate landscape-design positioning
  ("Kenya-based, serving clients across Kenya, with selected regional design
  briefs"), which remains unchanged.

## 4. Service structure (final approved names)

The following are the **final approved public names** (approved 12 July 2026).
They are no longer working names.

| Programme | Definition | Term & payment | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GardenCare Regular** | Weekly or fortnightly scheduled care for lawns and actively managed gardens. | Initial minimum three-month term, then rolling monthly; invoiced and paid **monthly in advance**. | For gardens needing frequent mowing and active bed management. |
| **GardenCare Monthly** | One comprehensive monthly visit for established, lower-maintenance gardens. | Initial minimum three-month term, then rolling monthly; invoiced and paid **monthly in advance**. | For established gardens that do not need frequent visits. |
| **GardenCare Seasonal** | Quarterly assessment and corrective care. | Scheduled quarterly and **paid per scheduled visit** (before each visit). No three-month/rolling-monthly term. | **Must NOT be presented as sufficient routine lawn maintenance.** It is an assessment/corrective offering, not a substitute for regular mowing/upkeep. |

Notes:

- The exact activities, frequency and durations within each programme are
  **scope-defined per property** at assessment — they are not fixed package
  contents (see §6 and §7).
- No visit durations, labour hours, crew sizes or number-of-staff figures are
  defined. These are deliberately omitted and must not be invented.
- **Recurring vs Seasonal:** GardenCare **Regular** and **Monthly** are the
  recurring programmes governed by the three-month initial term and rolling-monthly
  continuation (§9). GardenCare **Seasonal** is a per-scheduled-visit quarterly
  offering — it is not on a three-month/rolling-monthly term and is not routine
  lawn maintenance.

## 5. Qualification and assessment process

1. **Enquiry** — customer contacts Botanique (via the existing project-enquiry /
   WhatsApp channels; no new public GardenCare entry point in this phase).
2. **Coverage check** — confirm the property is within the launch coverage area
   (Nairobi Metropolitan Area, subject to accessibility/route availability).
3. **Site assessment** — a garden and location assessment is carried out to
   understand the garden type, size, condition, access, and the customer's
   expectations, using the practice's existing charged site-assessment process.
   This phase does **not** change the existing site-visit fee or payment
   calculations, and does not introduce a new assessment price.
4. **Scope definition** — from the assessment, define the property-specific scope:
   which routine activities apply, at what frequency, and which items are
   separately quoted.
5. **Proposal & written agreement** — present a property-specific scope and a
   custom price; the programme runs under a defined written agreement (§9).
6. **Assessment-fee credit** — the existing GardenCare/site-assessment fee is
   **credited toward the first GardenCare invoice** when the client accepts the
   maintenance agreement **within 14 calendar days of the assessment**. This does
   not change the website's existing site-visit fee calculation and does not create
   a new assessment price.
7. **Onboarding & recurring service** — schedule and begin service per the agreed
   scope, with a short visit summary after each visit (§17).

## 6. Potentially included routine activities

The following activities **may** form part of a GardenCare agreement **when they
are within the scope agreed for that specific property**. They are a menu of
possible routine work, **not** a promise that every activity is included in every
visit or every programme:

- lawn mowing and edging
- routine hedge and shrub pruning
- bed weeding
- deadheading
- routine garden cleanup
- plant-health observation
- basic irrigation observation
- agreed feeding or agreed seasonal tasks

Explicitly:

- **Do not promise that every activity is included in every visit or package.**
  Inclusions are defined per property and per programme at assessment.
- "Observation" (plant-health and irrigation) means visual monitoring and
  flagging — it does **not** imply treatment, repair or corrective work, which are
  separately quoted (§7).

## 7. Exclusions and separately quoted work

The following are **not** included in a standard GardenCare agreement and are
**separately quoted / require separate approval**:

- fertiliser, pesticides and specialist treatments (as materials/application)
- replacement plants
- soil, mulch and other materials
- transport outside the agreed operating area
- irrigation repairs or new irrigation work
- garden redesign and new installations
- major lawn rehabilitation
- tree surgery, hazardous tree work, or work needing specialist access equipment
- waste removal beyond ordinary agreed garden cleanup
- emergency or unscheduled work

No exclusion above may be reframed as automatically included. In particular there
is **no automatic plant replacement**, **no guaranteed plant survival**, **no
guaranteed pest elimination**, and **no unlimited labour**.

## 8. Pricing principles

- GardenCare is **custom-priced after garden and location assessment**.
- There are **no generic public prices** at this stage.
- Do **not** invent package prices, discounts, visit durations, labour hours,
  crew sizes, material markups or procurement fees.
- This phase does **not** change the website's existing site-visit fee or payment
  calculations, and introduces no new assessment price.
- **Assessment-fee credit (approved):** the initial assessment fee is credited
  toward the first GardenCare invoice when the client accepts the agreement within
  14 calendar days of the assessment (§5).

## 9. Agreement duration, payment cycle and cancellation (approved)

**Agreement duration**

- GardenCare **Regular** and **Monthly** have an **initial minimum agreement
  period of three months**. After the initial term, the agreement **continues on a
  rolling monthly basis**.
- GardenCare **Seasonal** is scheduled **quarterly and paid per scheduled visit**.
  It is not on a three-month/rolling-monthly term and is not presented as routine
  lawn maintenance.

**Renewal and cancellation**

- After the initial three-month term, GardenCare Regular and Monthly continue
  monthly.
- The client may end the rolling agreement by giving **30 days' written notice**.
- Do **not** claim "cancel anytime."
- Any termination **during** the initial three-month commitment remains **subject
  to the signed agreement**.

**Payment cycle**

- GardenCare Regular and Monthly are **invoiced and paid monthly in advance**.
- GardenCare Seasonal is **paid before each scheduled quarterly visit**.
- Separately approved materials and extraordinary work are invoiced **according to
  the accepted quotation**.
- Work does **not** begin or continue for an unpaid service period unless Botanique
  explicitly agrees otherwise in writing.

## 10. VAT (approved)

> **VAT is applied only where legally applicable and is shown clearly on the
> relevant proposal or invoice.**

Do **not** state or imply Botanique's current VAT-registration status unless it is
independently confirmed by the owner.

## 11. Weather and rescheduling (approved)

- When weather makes the work unsafe or materially impractical, the visit is moved
  to the **next practical available date**.
- A weather reschedule does **not** cause the client to lose the agreed visit.
- Do **not** promise a fixed replacement date regardless of conditions or route
  availability.

## 12. Property access (approved)

- The client must provide **confirmed access to the property at the scheduled
  time**.
- The client must provide **reasonable access to water** where the agreed
  maintenance activities require it.
- Any special security, estate-entry or key-handover arrangements must be **agreed
  before service begins**.

## 13. Client-caused missed visits (approved)

- A visit prevented by the client **without at least 24 hours' notice counts as a
  scheduled visit**.
- Any replacement visit **may be separately charged** and remains **subject to
  route availability**.
- This rule does **not** apply when Botanique caused the missed visit.

## 14. Waste removal (approved)

- Ordinary garden waste produced by the agreed routine activities is **consolidated
  at an agreed location onsite**.
- **Offsite transport, disposal and substantial waste removal are separately
  quoted.**
- Do **not** promise unlimited waste removal.

## 15. Materials and consumables (approved)

- Separately charged fertiliser, pesticides, plants, soil, mulch and other
  materials require **client approval through WhatsApp, email or another written
  channel before purchase**.
- Materials, delivery and specialist application are **charged separately** unless
  the signed proposal expressly includes them.
- Do **not** invent a material markup or procurement fee.

## 16. Emergency and unscheduled work (approved)

- Emergency, urgent and unscheduled work is **outside the standard GardenCare
  agreement**.
- It is subject to **team availability, assessment and a separate quotation**.
- GardenCare does **not** promise an emergency-response time.

## 17. Visit reporting and summaries (approved)

- After each visit, a **factual visit summary** is provided.
- The **assigned team lead prepares** the factual visit summary; the **Operations
  Manager oversees its delivery** to the client.
- The **initial delivery channel is WhatsApp**.
- A **simple internal visit checklist** may be used to support consistent reporting
  (internal operational aid only).
- Do **not** promise a client portal, live dashboard, or automated digital
  maintenance log.
- This phase does **not** modify the existing admin tracker, Supabase, or
  operations systems.

## 18. Operational workflow (enquiry → recurring service)

1. Enquiry received (existing channels).
2. Coverage/accessibility check against the launch coverage area.
3. Charged site assessment (existing process/fee).
4. Property-specific scope + custom quote prepared.
5. Written agreement signed; **advance payment** received (monthly in advance for
   Regular/Monthly; before each visit for Seasonal). The assessment fee is credited
   to the first invoice if the agreement is accepted within 14 days (§5).
6. Schedule set (per the chosen programme structure and term, §9).
7. Recurring service performed to the agreed scope.
8. Short WhatsApp visit summary after each visit — team lead prepares, Operations
   Manager oversees delivery (§17).
9. Separately-quoted items (materials, extraordinary work) approved in writing
   before being carried out (§15).
10. Renewal continues monthly after the initial three-month term; the client may
    end the rolling agreement with 30 days' written notice (§9).

## 19. Boundaries against design, installation and specialist work

GardenCare is maintenance of an existing garden to an agreed scope. It does **not**
include, and must not be conflated with:

- landscape/garden **design** or planting design
- landscape **architecture**
- **implementation/construction** or new installations
- garden **redesign**
- **major lawn rehabilitation**
- **irrigation** design, installation or repair
- **tree surgery** / hazardous tree work / specialist access work
- specialist horticultural **treatments** (pest/disease treatment programmes,
  soil remediation, etc.)

Where a GardenCare customer needs any of the above, it is handled as a separate,
separately-quoted engagement under the relevant Botanique service — not folded
into the maintenance agreement.

## 20. Claims that must NOT be published

The following must not appear in any GardenCare public copy (now or at launch)
unless a specific decision explicitly authorises it:

- invented package prices, discounts, visit durations, labour hours, crew sizes,
  material markups or procurement fees
- "cancel anytime" (the approved cancellation terms are the three-month initial
  term plus 30 days' written notice on the rolling agreement — §9)
- any minimum contract duration other than the approved three-month initial term
- nationwide or blanket three-county coverage (coverage is the specific Nairobi
  Metropolitan Area wording in §3)
- automatic plant replacement, guaranteed plant survival, or guaranteed pest
  elimination
- unlimited labour, unlimited waste removal, or "everything included" style
  promises
- a promise that every listed activity is included in every visit/package
- a client portal, live dashboard, or automated digital maintenance log
- crew/team-consistency guarantees (e.g. "the same consistent team every visit")
  unless operationally committed and approved
- a fixed weather-replacement date regardless of conditions or route availability
- an emergency-response-time promise
- any statement of Botanique's VAT-registration status that the owner has not
  independently confirmed
